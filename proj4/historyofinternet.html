What is ARPA & Arpanet

The Advanced Research Projects Agency Network (ARPANET) was the first wide-area packet-switched network with distributed control and one of the first networks to implement the TCP/IP protocol suite. Both technologies became the technical foundation of the Internet. The ARPANET was established by the Advanced Research Projects Agency (ARPA) of the United States Department of Defense. It was developed during the Cold War and was ready at the end of 1969. Back to the Top

Tim Berners - Lee

Sir Timothy John Berners-Lee, born 8 June 1955, also known as TimBL, is an English computer scientist best known as the inventor of the World Wide Web (www). He is a Professorial Fellow of Computer Science at the University of Oxford and a professor at the Massachusetts Institute of Technology (MIT).Berners-Lee proposed an information management system on 12 March 1989, then implemented the first successful communication between a Hyper Text Transfer Protocol (HTTP) client and server via the Internet in mid-November. What is BBS's

BBS (Bulletin Board System) is a computer server software that allows users to connect to the system while using a terminal program. BBS is used for sharing or exchanging messages, files, data etc. on a network. BBS was primary king of online community before World Wide Web (www) arrived. Back to the Top

What is Fidonet

FidoNet is a computer network for personal computers. Having the first high speed internet connection is was used worldwide and FidoNet did it by using BBS's as its main functions.
